QR Code Review System
What is a Google Review QR Code?
A Google Review QR Code is a scannable code that, when scanned with any smartphone camera, directs the customer through ReviewDock's smart funnel - first to a rating prompt, then either to your Google review page or a private feedback form based on their rating.
How do I generate a QR code with ReviewDock?

Yes. ReviewDock allows you to customize your QR code with your brand colors and logo to ensure it looks professional on signage, menus, receipts, and other printed materials.
What is a Dynamic QR Code?
A dynamic QR code can be updated after printing. If you change your review link or destination URL, the physical QR code does not need to be reprinted - the destination updates automatically. ReviewDock uses dynamic QR codes for maximum flexibility.
What is a Static QR Code?
A static QR code encodes a fixed URL that cannot be changed after creation. If your review link changes, you must generate and reprint a new static QR code.
How large should I print my ReviewDock QR code?
For reliable scanning, print your QR code at a minimum of 1.5 x 1.5 inches (approximately 3.8 x 3.8 cm). For wall displays or table tents, larger is better - 3 to 6 inches is ideal.
Where should I place my review QR codes?
Place QR codes wherever customers are most satisfied and most likely to engage: on dining tables, at checkout counters, on receipts, on service completion invoices, in waiting rooms, on product packaging, on business cards, and in post-purchase emails.
Can I create different QR codes for different locations?
Yes. ReviewDock supports multiple business locations. You can generate separate QR codes for each location, each linked to its specific Google Business Profile, and track them independently in the dashboard.
Can I track how many times my QR code has been scanned?
Yes. ReviewDock's analytics dashboard shows scan data, including the number of scans, time and date of scans, device types, and the conversion rate from scan to completed review.
Does the QR code work on all smartphones?
Yes. Modern iPhone and Android camera apps natively scan QR codes without needing a separate app. ReviewDock QR codes are compatible with all major devices and operating systems.
Can I use ReviewDock's QR code in digital formats, not just print?

Google Business Profile & Local SEO
Why are Google reviews so important for local businesses?
Google reviews are one of the most heavily weighted factors in Google's local search ranking algorithm. Businesses with more recent, positive reviews rank higher in Google Maps and local search results, directly translating to more customer inquiries and revenue.
How many Google reviews does a business need to rank well?
There is no magic number, but consistency matters. In most local markets, businesses with 50+ reviews and a rating above 4.3 stars tend to rank significantly higher than competitors. In competitive industries, 100-500+ reviews may be needed to dominate local search.
Does the recency of reviews affect Google rankings?
Yes. Google's algorithm weights recent reviews more heavily than old ones. A consistent flow of new reviews signals an active, reliable business. ReviewDock helps maintain this steady stream.
What star rating should I aim for on Google?
Aim for a rating above 4.3 stars. Studies show consumer trust drops significantly below 4.0, and Google itself tends to feature businesses with higher ratings in its local pack results.
How do more Google reviews help attract new customers?
93% of consumers read online reviews before making a purchasing decision. A business with 200+ positive Google reviews appears significantly more credible and trustworthy than one with 10, even if the service quality is identical.
Does ReviewDock help with Google Maps ranking specifically?
Yes. By systematically growing your review count and maintaining a high star rating, ReviewDock directly contributes to the signals Google Maps uses to rank local businesses in the '3-pack' (the top three businesses shown for local searches).
